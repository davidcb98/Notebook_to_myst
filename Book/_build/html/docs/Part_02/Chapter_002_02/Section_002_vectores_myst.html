    Demostraci贸n
    \[\begin{align*} \braket{e_k}{v} &= \bra{e_k}\left(\sum_{j=1}^N v_j\ket{e_j}\right) \nonumber\\ &= \sum_{j=1}^N v_j\braket{e_k}{e_j} \nonumber\\ &= \sum_{j=1}^N v_j\delta_{kj} = v_k \end{align*}\]

  • Calcular el valor de un producto escalar \(a=\braket{u}{v}\) es muy simple si conocemos las componentes de \(\ket{u}\) y \(\ket{v}\) en una base ortonormal:

    \[\begin{equation*} a = \braket{u}{v} = \left(\sum_{i}u_i^*\bra{e_i}\right)\left(\sum_{j}v_j\ket{e_j} \right) = \sum_{ij} u_i^* v_j \braket{e_i}{e_j} = \sum_{ij} u_i^* v_j \delta_{ij} = \sum_{i} u_i^* v_i ~~ \Rightarrow \\ \end{equation*}\]
    (2.66)#\[\begin{equation} \Rightarrow \boxed{a = \braket{u}{v} = \begin{bmatrix} {u_1^*} & {u_2^*} & \cdots & {u_N^*} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} {v_1}\\ {v_2}\\ \vdots \\ {v_N} \end{bmatrix}} \end{equation}\]
